Ginataang Alimasag with Sitaw at Kalabasa

The phrase “Ginataang Alimasag” refers to crabs cooked in sweet, tasty coconut milk. Filipinos love anything with gata or coconut milk –– you could say we go nuts for coconuts! It’s one of our most abundant produce, especially since we live in a tropical country.

prep time

4 mins

cook time

30 mins

serving

3 servings

Tags : vegetable

instructions

step 1

Heat oil in a pan.

step 2

Saute garlic, onion, and ginger until onion turns translucent.

step 3

Combine water and Knorr Ginataang Gulay Mix. Stir until well blended. Pour mixture into the pan. Let boil.

step 4

Add crab and kalabasa. Cover and boil for 5 minutes.

step 5

Add snake beans. Continue cooking for 8 minutes.

step 6

Season with salt and pepper if desired.

step 7

Share and Enjoy!

ingredients

3 pieces crab steamed for 12 minutes

40 grams Knorr Ginataang Gulay Recipe Mix

15 pieces snake beans cut into 2-inch pieces

12 ounces calabaza squash cubed

1 piece onion chopped

2 thumbs ginger minced

5 cloves garlic crushed and chopped

3 tablespoons cooking oil

2 1/2 cups water room temperature

Salt and ground black pepper to taste optional

tools

Large pot or deep pan with a lid

Wooden spoon or spatula

Measuring cups and spoons

Bowl

Colander

Tongs
